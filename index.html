CoachObey

PWA / running / adaptive AI coaching workflow

CoachObey is a mobile-first race-prep app for recreational half marathon and marathon runners. The product is designed for runners who want more than a static training plan, but may not be ready to hire a human coach.

The MVP focuses on a practical coaching loop: understand the runner’s race goal and training background, generate an initial plan, then help the runner stay on track as real life interferes. The app is intended to handle common training disruptions like missed runs, fatigue, soreness, travel, work stress, calendar conflicts, and uncertainty about what to adjust next.

I’m building this as a PWA first so I can validate the product behavior and coaching workflow before investing in a native mobile app. The current build is also a way to explore how LLMs can be applied inside a structured product system rather than used as a generic chatbot.

Core product loop

  • Sign in with Google
  • Choose or manually enter a race
  • Complete a streamlined race-prep onboarding flow
  • Enter running background, goal, availability, and injury history
  • Assess current training capacity and race readiness
  • Generate an initial conservative plan
  • Choose a coaching personality
  • Check in before runs and log completed training
  • Compare actual training against the plan
  • Ask the coach for guidance when training gets disrupted
  • Receive missed-workout guidance and weekly training reviews

Adaptive coaching layer

CoachObey is not positioned as a general running chatbot. The coach chat is scoped to race preparation, training decisions, recovery, accountability, and plan guidance.

  • Answers questions using the runner’s race, plan, and recent training context
  • Helps interpret bad workouts, missed runs, fatigue, and schedule conflicts
  • Explains why a workout matters and what to focus on next
  • Can propose plan adjustments when availability or readiness changes
  • Turns validated plan changes into clear runner-facing guidance
  • Maintains lightweight coaching memory around preferences and recurring patterns

Product direction

A key product idea is that runners respond to different types of coaching. The app supports selectable coach personalities that change the tone and accountability style without changing the underlying safety rules.

  • Knox: direct, demanding, and accountability-heavy
  • Mara: calm, practical, and steady
  • Sunny: encouraging and confidence-building
  • Atlas: analytical and data-driven

The intention is to make the coach feel more useful and personal while keeping the product bounded as a race-prep tool, not a general-purpose AI companion.

Rules, evals, and architecture

The LLM is not the training engine. Server-side rules own plan structure, race-distance logic, training-capacity tiers, workout permissions, safety guardrails, quota checks, and viable adjustment options. The LLM helps interpret messy runner input and translate approved decisions into concise, useful coaching.

Plan changes are handled through a patch workflow: the LLM can propose a structured adjustment, but deterministic validators decide whether the change is safe and allowed before the plan is updated.

  • Does not diagnose injuries
  • Does not recommend training through sharp, worsening, or gait-changing pain
  • Does not suggest cramming missed mileage
  • Does not add punishment workouts
  • Preserves long-run importance for marathon plans
  • Keeps guidance distance-specific
  • Trains from current fitness rather than unsupported goal pace
  • Requires plan changes to match server-approved options
  • Gives the runner a clear next action

I’m also building an evaluation harness around the coaching layer. The goal is to test whether generated guidance is safe, conservative where needed, specific to the runner, aligned with deterministic training rules, and consistent with the selected coach personality.
